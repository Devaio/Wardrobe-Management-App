THE TYRANNY OF CHOICE

Since the early-2000s, a growing body of research* has shown that rather than contributing to more happiness, the increasing availability of choices actually causes greater stress and anxiety. These adverse effects can be far-reaching, from buying groceries at the supermarket to running a Fortune 500 corporation.

GREY T-SHIRTS AND BLACK TURTLENECKS

Steve Jobs was legendary for his black mock turtlenecks, blue jeans, and New Balance sneakers. Mark Zuckerberg has built a similar notoriety around his closet full of grey t-shirts. President Obama famously admitted that his wardrobe only contains blue or grey suits.

NOT JUST FOR EXECUTIVES

Although once viewed as the eccentricity of quirky tech billionaires, more and more people are realizing that what you wear doesn't have to define you. Minimizing the things that don't matter actually can free you to be happier and lead a more effective life.
